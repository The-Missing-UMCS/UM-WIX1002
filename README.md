### 1.1. Year 1 (Last updated: 17/12/2022)

December 2022 was one of the most challenging times in my programming journey. I lost every single Lab and Tutorial file in Eclipse. I realized this devastating loss on 14/12/2022, and it hit me hard because I had no backups. The only silver lining was the few files I had pushed to GitHub/GitLab (Lab1 to Lab6).

The lab questions were tough‚Äîbrutally tough. Problem Solving 3 was no exception. It took me over 12 hours to redo everything, but by 17/12/2022, I had finally completed it all. That moment was filled with relief and pride.

One key takeaway from this ordeal: **NEVER GIVE UP.** Programming isn‚Äôt about getting it right the first time. It‚Äôs normal to spend 30 minutes, an hour, or even an entire day wrestling with one question. I remember spending two hours debugging and modifying `L10Q4.java`.

I tried using `LinkedBlockingQueue` and `PriorityQueue` in `L10Q4.java` to maintain appointments in ascending order. But it didn‚Äôt work because:

> "The Iterator provided in method iterator() is not guaranteed to traverse the elements of the priority queue in any particular order." ‚Äî *Java Documentation*

It took countless attempts and over three hours to figure this out. While it was frustrating, it taught me resilience and the importance of understanding the tools I use.

Here are some tips that helped me through:

1. Explore [StackOverflow](https://stackoverflow.com/). You can find answers to almost anything there.
2. Use [Google](https://www.google.com/) extensively. It‚Äôs often more powerful than you realize.
3. Ask questions on [ChatGPT](https://openai.com/blog/chatgpt/). While incredibly helpful, don‚Äôt over-rely on it or let AI limit your creativity.
4. Read others‚Äô code on [GitHub](https://github.com/) or [GitLab](https://gitlab.com/). As programmers, we learn by reading.
5. Dive into the [Java Documentation](https://docs.oracle.com/javase/7/docs/api/). It‚Äôs an invaluable resource.
6. Don‚Äôt hesitate to seek advice from peers, mentors, or friends who excel at programming.

If you‚Äôre ever struggling, remember that it‚Äôs part of the process. Feel free to reach out to me at 017-780 9298 (WhatsApp/Telegram). You‚Äôre not alone, and you‚Äôve got this. Good luck!
